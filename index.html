Gulab jabun

Ingredients

  • Milk solids (khoya or milk powder)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Baking powder
  • Ghee (for dough/frying)
  • Milk (for kneading)
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Cardamom
  • Rose water

Recipe

To make Gulab Jamun, first prepare a sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, cardamom, and rose water until slightly sticky. Next, create a soft, crack-free dough by combining milk solids (khoya or milk powder), a small amount of all-purpose flour, baking powder, and ghee, kneading gently with a little milk. Form small, smooth balls from the dough, then deep-fry them slowly in hot oil or ghee until golden brown. Finally, immediately transfer the warm, fried jamuns into the prepared warm sugar syrup and let them soak for several hours, allowing them to absorb the sweetness and become soft and plump.

Barfi

Ingredients

  • Milk solids (khoya or milk powder)
  • Nuts (optional, for garnish/texture)
  • Ghee
  • Sugar
  • Cardamom

Recipe

To prepare Barfi, begin by heating ghee in a heavy-bottomed pan. Add milk solids (khoya or milk powder) and sugar, stirring continuously over low to medium heat until the mixture thickens and starts to leave the sides of the pan. If using, incorporate cardamom powder for flavor. Once the mixture reaches a fudge-like consistency, spread it evenly onto a greased tray. Allow it to cool and set completely at room temperature, then cut into desired shapes like squares or diamonds. Optionally, garnish with chopped nuts before serving.

Balu-Shahi

Ingredients

  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Yogurt
  • Baking soda (or baking powder)
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Cardamom (optional, for syrup)
  • Saffron (optional, for syrup)
  • Oil/Ghee (for frying)
  • Saffron (optional, for flavor/color)

Recipe

To make Balushahi, first prepare a thick sugar syrup infused with cardamom and saffron. Next, create a flaky dough by gently mixing all-purpose flour with melted ghee, curd, and a small amount of baking powder/soda, ensuring not to over-knead. Form the dough into flattened discs with a central indentation, then slowly deep-fry these in warm ghee or oil until they turn golden brown and develop distinct layers. Finally, immediately transfer the hot fried Balushahi into the prepared warm sugar syrup, allowing them to soak thoroughly until they become sweet and juicy from within.

Besan Ladoo

Ingredients

  • Besan (gram flour)
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Powdered sugar (boora or tagar)
  • Cardamom powder (optional)
  • Nuts (optional, for garnish/texture)

Recipe

To make Besan Ladoo, begin by dry roasting besan (gram flour) in a heavy-bottomed pan until fragrant and lightly browned. Gradually add ghee to the roasted besan, continuing to stir and cook on low heat until the mixture turns golden and a nutty aroma fills the air. Remove from heat and let it cool slightly. Once lukewarm, thoroughly mix in powdered sugar and cardamom powder if desired. Finally, while the mixture is still warm, take small portions and firmly press them into smooth, round ladoos.

Kaju katli

Ingredients

  • Cashews (kaju)
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Ghee (for greasing, optional)
  • Cardamom powder (optional)
  • Edible silver leaf (chandi vark, optional for garnish)

Recipe

To make Kaju Katli, start by grinding cashews into a fine powder or smooth paste. In a separate pan, prepare a sugar syrup by boiling sugar with water until it reaches a one-string consistency. Add the cashew powder/paste to the hot syrup and mix continuously on low heat until the mixture thickens and begins to leave the sides of the pan. Transfer this warm mixture to a greased surface or parchment paper and knead gently until smooth. Roll it out evenly to a desired thickness, optionally applying edible silver leaf, then cut into traditional diamond shapes before allowing to cool and set.

Modak

Ingredients

  • Rice flour
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Ghee (for greasing, optional)
  • Cardamom powder (optional)
  • Freshly grated coconut
  • Jaggery
  • Nutmeg powder (optional)
  • Poppy seeds (optional)

Recipe

To make Modak, first prepare the sweet filling (puran) by cooking grated coconut and jaggery together until thickened, adding cardamom and nutmeg for flavor. For the outer shell, boil water with a pinch of salt and ghee, then add rice flour and mix to form a dough. Knead the dough until smooth. Flatten small portions of the dough into discs, place a spoonful of the filling in the center, and carefully pleat and seal to form a modak shape. Finally, steam the modaks for about 10-15 minutes until glossy and firm, serving them hot with ghee.

Rasmalai

Ingredients

  • Full-fat milk (for chenna)
  • Lemon juice or vinegar (for curdling milk)
  • Sugar
  • Ghee (for greasing, optional)
  • Green cardamom pods or powder
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Pistachios or almonds (for garnish, optional)

Recipe

To make Rasmalai, first curdle full-fat milk with lemon juice or vinegar to create chenna. Drain and knead the chenna until smooth, then form small, flattened discs. Boil these discs in a light sugar syrup until they swell and become spongy. Simultaneously, prepare the malai by simmering milk with sugar, saffron, and cardamom until it thickens slightly. Gently squeeze the excess syrup from the cooked chenna discs and transfer them into the warm, sweetened milk. Let them soak for a few hours, ideally chilling them, allowing the discs to absorb the rich, flavored milk before serving, garnished with nuts.

Sonpapdi

Ingredients

  • Besan (gram flour)
  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Cardamom powder (optional)
  • Pistachios/Almonds (for garnish, optional)

Recipe

To make Soan Papdi, first prepare a sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water until it reaches a brittle, hard-ball consistency. In parallel, roast a mixture of besan and all-purpose flour in ample ghee until golden and fragrant. Once the sugar syrup is ready, pour it onto a clean, greased surface and allow it to cool slightly. Then, add the roasted flour mixture to the semi-cooled syrup and, using a metal scraper or your hands (once cool enough), continuously fold and pull the mixture to create fine, thread-like layers. Once flaky, press it into a greased tray, flatten, and cut into squares before it completely sets.

Gajar ka halwa

Ingredients

  • Carrots (grated)
  • Ghee (clarified butter)
  • Milk (full-fat)
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Khoya (milk solids, optional)
  • Cardamom powder
  • Nuts (almonds, cashews, pistachios - for garnish)
  • Raisins (optional)

Recipe

To make Gajar Ka Halwa, begin by grating fresh carrots. In a large, heavy-bottomed pan, sauté the grated carrots in ghee for a few minutes until slightly softened. Next, add full-fat milk and continue to cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until all the milk has been absorbed and evaporated. Then, stir in sugar and cook until it dissolves and the mixture thickens. For a richer taste, add khoya (milk solids) and cook for a few more minutes. Finally, mix in cardamom powder and garnish with roasted nuts and raisins before serving warm.

Black-gulabjabun

Ingredients

  • Milk solids (khoya or milk powder)
  • All-purpose flour (maida)
  • Baking powder
  • Ghee (for dough/frying)
  • Milk (for kneading)
  • Sugar
  • Water
  • Cardamom (for syrup and/or dough)
  • Rose water (for syrup)

Recipe

To make Black Gulab Jamun, first prepare a sugar syrup infused with cardamom and rose water. For the jamuns, create a soft, crack-free dough using milk solids (khoya or milk powder), a small amount of all-purpose flour, baking powder, and ghee, kneading gently with milk. Form small, smooth balls from the dough. The crucial step for the "black" color is to deep-fry these balls slowly over a low to medium flame for a longer duration than regular gulab jamuns, allowing them to turn a deep, almost blackish-brown and develop a slightly crusty exterior. Once fried, immediately transfer the hot jamuns into the prepared warm sugar syrup and let them soak for several hours until they swell and become juicy.
